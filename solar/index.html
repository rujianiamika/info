Celestial Bodies Information

Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of our Solar System. It rotates on its axis once in about 25-35 days.

Diameter: 1,392,700 km (109 times Earth's diameter)

Earth

Earth orbits the Sun once every 365.25 days and rotates on its axis once every 24 hours.

Diameter: 12,742 km

Distance from Sun: 149.6 million km

Moon

The Moon orbits Earth once every 27.3 days and rotates on its axis at the same rate, which is why we always see the same side.

Diameter: 3,474 km

Distance from Earth: 384,400 km

Interesting Facts

  • The Earth rotates at about 1,670 km/h at the equator.
  • The Moon is moving away from Earth at a rate of 3.8 cm per year.
  • The Sun contains 99.86% of the mass in the Solar System.
  • The Earth's orbit around the Sun is not a perfect circle but an ellipse.
  • The Moon's synchronous rotation means it takes the same amount of time to rotate on its axis as it does to orbit Earth.
